# Foundation ADR Format Contract

This document specifies the exact format that all Foundation ADRs (Architecture Decision Records)
must follow when generated by the Foundation Orchestration system.

Every ADR generated by `adr_generator.py` must conform to this contract exactly.

---

## File Naming Convention

```
docs/adr/{0-padded-sequence}-{kebab-case-title}.md

Examples:
  docs/adr/0000-runtime-selection.md
  docs/adr/0001-framework-choice.md
  docs/adr/0002-architecture-style.md
```

---

## Required Frontmatter (YAML)

```yaml
---
status: Proposed | Accepted | Superseded | Rejected
date: YYYY-MM-DD
author: Generated by Foundation Orchestrator (or manual)
decision_area: runtime | framework | architecture-style | data | deployment | testing | other
affects: [component1, component2]
relates_to: [foundation-issue-number, related-adr-numbers]
---
```

---

## Markdown Structure & Requirements

### 1. Title

```markdown
# ADR-{number}: {Decision Title}

**Status:** {status}  
**Date:** {date}  
**Author:** {author}  
**Decision Area:** {area}  
**Affected Components:** [list]  
**Relates To:** [issue numbers / other ADRs]
```

**Validation Rules:**
- Title must be specific and actionable (not "Architecture" but "Choose-monolith-vs-services")
- Title must be in kebab-case when used in filename
- Status must be one of: Proposed, Accepted, Superseded, Rejected

---

### 2. Context

**Purpose:** Why this decision matters, what forces drive it, what assumptions exist.

**Requirements:**
- 150–400 words (not too brief, not a novel)
- 2-3 sentence problem statement referencing FOUNDATION.md constraints
- Must identify 2-4 decision forces
- Each force must explain why it matters to this decision

**Template:**
```markdown
## Context

[2-3 sentence problem statement referencing FOUNDATION.md constraints]

### Forces

1. **[Force Name]**: [Description and why it matters to this decision]
2. **[Force Name]**: [Description and why it matters]
3. **[Force Name]**: [Description and why it matters]

*Reference: FOUNDATION.md § [section name]*
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ Problem statement present (2-3 sentences)
- ✅ Forces section present with 2-4 forces
- ✅ Each force has explanation tied to project constraints
- ✅ FOUNDATION.md reference present

---

### 3. Decision

**Purpose:** The chosen option, stated clearly and specifically.

**Requirements:**
- Single paragraph, 1-2 sentences max
- Concrete choice, not "further evaluation needed"
- Rationale goes in next section, NOT here
- Must be a real decision, not a question or conditional

**Example (GOOD):**
"We will use PostgreSQL as the primary data store for application state, with Redis for session caching and rate-limiting."

**Example (BAD - Reject):**
"We should consider database options like PostgreSQL or MongoDB, depending on team preference."

**Template:**
```markdown
## Decision

[Concrete chosen option in clear language]
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ Decision is specific and concrete
- ✅ Uses "will" not "should" or "may"
- ✅ No hedging language ("consider", "evaluate")

---

### 4. Alternatives Considered

**Purpose:** Realistic options evaluated, with genuine tradeoffs (not straw men).

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 2 alternatives (typically 3-4)
- For each: name, brief description, 2+ pros, 2+ cons
- Show WHY chosen option was preferred
- Be honest about tradeoffs; no hiding information

**Template:**
```markdown
## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: [Name]

**Description:** [Brief summary]

**Pros:**
- [Genuine advantage with context]
- [Genuine advantage with context]

**Cons:**
- [Real drawback with severity: why it matters]
- [Real drawback]

### Alternative 2: [Name]

[Same structure]

### Alternative 3: [Name]

[Same structure]

### Why We Chose [Chosen Option]

[1-2 sentences explaining the decision criteria that made chosen option best fit]
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ Minimum 2 alternatives with names
- ✅ Each has description, 2+ pros, 2+ cons
- ✅ Pros/cons are realistic (not marketing speak)
- ✅ "Why we chose" section explains the reasoning

---

### 5. Rationale

**Purpose:** Detailed explanation of why this choice fits the project constraints and goals.

**Requirements:**
- Tie back to Context/Forces explicitly
- Reference team skill, budget, timeline, architecture needs from FOUNDATION.md
- Explain how this aligns with other foundation decisions
- 3–5 numbered reasons, each 2–3 sentences
- Minimum 200 words

**Template:**
```markdown
## Rationale

### 1. [Criteria Name]

[2-3 sentences explaining how chosen option excels on this criterion, tying back to forces]

### 2. [Criteria Name]

[2-3 sentences]

### 3. [Criteria Name]

[2-3 sentences]
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ 3–5 numbered reasons present
- ✅ Each reason ties back to Context forces
- ✅ Each reason references FOUNDATION.md constraints
- ✅ Total 200+ words

---

### 6. Consequences

**Purpose:** What happens as a result of this decision (good and bad).

**Requirements:**
- Must list actual consequences, not speculation
- Separate Positive and Negative / Risks sections
- Minimum 2 positive, 2 negative outcomes
- Be honest about downsides
- Negative risks should include severity level

**Template:**
```markdown
## Consequences

### Positive

- ✅ [Real upside: what benefit, why does it matter?]
- ✅ [Real upside]
- ✅ [Real upside]

### Negative / Risks

- ⚠️ **[Risk Name]** (Severity: LOW | MEDIUM | HIGH)  
  [Brief description of what could go wrong and impact]

- ⚠️ **[Risk Name]** (Severity: LOW | MEDIUM | HIGH)  
  [Description]
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ Positive section present with 2+ items
- ✅ Negative section present with 2+ items
- ✅ Risks marked with severity level
- ✅ No hidden downsides

---

### 7. Validation Strategy

**Purpose:** How do we know this decision is working?

**Requirements:**
- Metrics that can be measured
- Thresholds that define "success" vs. "needs review"
- Review cadence (e.g., "monthly", "quarterly", "on-demand")
- Examples: uptime %, p95 latency, deployment frequency, team onboarding time

**Template:**
```markdown
## Validation Strategy

**Metrics:**
- [Measurable metric #1]: Success threshold = [number or range]
- [Measurable metric #2]: Success threshold = [number or range]

**Review Schedule:**
- Initial review: [date, e.g., 3 months from go-live]
- Cadence: [monthly | quarterly | on-demand]
- Escalation trigger: [condition that requires re-evaluation]

**Example:** If p95 query latency exceeds 500ms, or if data retention costs exceed $5K/month, re-evaluate the persistence strategy.
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ Minimum 2 metrics with thresholds
- ✅ Each metric is measurable (has numbers)
- ✅ Review cadence specified
- ✅ Escalation trigger defined

---

### 8. Rollback / Exit Strategy

**Purpose:** Exit strategy if the decision proves wrong.

**Requirements:**
- Concrete steps to undo or switch if needed
- Cost estimate (effort, downtime, risk)
- Conditions under which rollback is triggered

**Template:**
```markdown
## Rollback / Exit Strategy

**Conditions under which this decision might be reconsidered:**
[Specific, measurable conditions — e.g., "if team cannot maintain 99.9% availability"]

**Rollback steps:**
1. [Step 1 with concrete action]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

**Cost of rollback:**
- Effort: [engineer-days estimate]
- Downtime: [duration and impact]
- Risk: [what could break during rollback]

**Example:** Rolling back from PostgreSQL to SQLite would take ~3 engineer-days and require ~2 hours of read-only downtime. Risk: data loss if dump/restore is interrupted.
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ Rollback conditions are specific (not vague)
- ✅ Rollback steps are numbered and concrete
- ✅ Cost estimate provided (effort, downtime, risk)

---

### 9. Related Decisions

**Purpose:** Links to other foundation ADRs, constraints, and context.

**Requirements:**
- Links to other ADRs (dependencies and reverse dependencies)
- References to FOUNDATION.md sections
- References to research wiki and issue
- "Depends on", "Unlocks", "Supersedes", "Superseded by" sections

**Template:**
```markdown
## Related Decisions

**Depends on:**
- ADR-0000: [title] — [reason: e.g., "Must decide deployment platform before data store"]
- FOUNDATION.md § [Section]: [constraint this addresses]

**Unlocks:**
- ADR-TBD: [future decision this enables]

**Supersedes:**
- [Old ADR number / decision if this replaces prior art]

**Superseded by:**
- [Newer ADR if this has been replaced]

**Related constraints:**
- `FOUNDATION.md` § [Section Name]: [How this honors that constraint]
- `docs/discovery-focus.md`: [Which user outcome this supports]

**References:**
- Foundation research issue: #[number]
- Research wiki: wiki/foundation/[page-name].md
- Discovery focus: [link]
```

**Validation:**
- ✅ "Depends on" section present (if applicable)
- ✅ "Unlocks" section present (if applicable)
- ✅ References to FOUNDATION.md present
- ✅ Research issue and wiki links present

---

## Quality Standards (Enforced)

### Clarity
- Every section must be understandable to someone not involved in research
- Not jargon-heavy; not over-simplified
- Technical but readable

### Concreteness
- No hand-waving
- Metrics are numbers
- Tradeoffs are real, not fictional
- Examples included where helpful

### Coherence
- This ADR aligns with other foundation decisions
- No contradictions with FOUNDATION.md
- Cross-references between related ADRs

### Traceability
- Every claim tied back to evidence (wiki, FOUNDATION.md, research)
- Research issue and wiki links present
- Rationale clearly connects forces to decision

### Honesty
- Downsides and risks stated openly
- No hiding bad news
- Realistic assessment of consequences

---

## Validation Checklist (Pre-Commit)

Before an ADR is committed to docs/adr/:

- ✅ File is named `docs/adr/{0000-sequence}-{kebab-case-title}.md`
- ✅ Frontmatter (YAML) is present and valid
- ✅ All 9 sections present and non-empty
- ✅ Title is specific, not generic
- ✅ Context has problem statement + Forces (2-4)
- ✅ Decision is concrete (uses "will", not "may")
- ✅ Alternatives has 2+ options with pros/cons + explanation
- ✅ Rationale has 3-5 numbered reasons tying to forces
- ✅ Consequences has positive + negative/risks
- ✅ Validation has measurable metrics + thresholds + cadence
- ✅ Rollback has conditions + steps + cost estimate
- ✅ Related has links to other ADRs and FOUNDATION.md
- ✅ No contradictions with FOUNDATION.md
- ✅ No contradictions with other ADRs
- ✅ Markdown is clean (no formatting errors)
- ✅ Links are resolvable (to issues, wiki, FOUNDATION.md)
